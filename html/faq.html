General:


  • What is the best bonsai for a beginner?
    People new to the art of bonsai often ask us this question. The answer is - it depends! Success with bonsai depends on two important things: location and watering. Having said that, some species of tree are certainly more manageable than others.
    Bonsai trees are suitable for any home, and they are also great for someone who is not used to caring for a garden. When you start with bonsai, you tend to want to keep them in the house. After all, they are houseplants. You won't see them as much as you would if they were on the coffee table/desk. Some bonsai plants can be kept indoors, and people tend to start with these.
    •  
  • What is the meaning of bonsai?
    The word “Bon-sai” (often misspelled as Bonzai or Banzai) is a Japanese term that when translated, means “planted in a container”. This was derived from an ancient Chinese horticultural practice, which was then redeveloped under the influence of Japanese Zen Buddhism.
    It has been around for well over a thousand years. The ultimate goal of growing a Bonsai is to create a miniaturized as well as realistic representation of nature in the form of a tree. Bonsai are not genetically dwarfed plants any tree species can be used to grow a bonsai.
    •  
  • How often should I water my bonsai?
    Approximately once a week or so, when the topsoil feels completely dry, immerse the entire bonsai plant in a bucket or basin of water until all the air bubbles have risen to the top. Humidity is an important consideration for the health of the bonsai.
    •  
  • Why is bonsai so expensive?
    Bonsai creation is an art form. Taking care of a bonsai is very labor intensive and often requires skilled workers. There is a low supply of good quality bonsai. It is usually a long-term investment as they can survive for many years.
